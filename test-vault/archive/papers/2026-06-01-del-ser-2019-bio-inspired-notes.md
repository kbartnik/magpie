## Deep Read

**Key Insight:** The field's biggest unsolved problem isn't algorithm performance — it's lack of standardized benchmarks and comparative methodology. Without consensus on how to measure bio-inspired optimizers fairly, the literature can't reliably identify which approaches actually outperform classical methods on real-world problems.

**What Surprised Me:** The paper is authored by 10 researchers from 9 institutions across Spain, India, the UK, and Mexico — signaling a genuinely fragmented, internationally distributed field. The explicit call to "reach a consensus and join forces" reads as an admission that the community is pulling in different directions rather than building on shared foundations.

**Open Questions:**
- What progress has been made since 2019 on benchmarking consensus? The problem was identified clearly — did the community converge, or is the 2025 literature still comparatively uncoordinated?
- The paper covers multi-objective and many-objective optimization as separate areas — at what dimensionality does "many-objective" become necessary, and are there practical rules of thumb?
- Surrogate model-assisted optimization is listed as an open area; in 2025, DL models are the obvious candidate for surrogates. Has this integration happened?
