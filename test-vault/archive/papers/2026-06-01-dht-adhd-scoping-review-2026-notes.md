## Deep Read

**Key Insight:** The most comprehensive picture yet of the DHT landscape for adult ADHD: 133 studies across treatment (n=63), clinical management (n=36), and diagnosis (n=19). The evidence base is large but scattered — most studies are preliminary efficacy tests with high risk of bias. The field lacks clear frameworks for how DHTs integrate into existing clinical pathways.

**What Surprised Me:** UK wait times for adult ADHD diagnosis now commonly exceed **2 years**, and the administrative prevalence in GP records (0.3% for men, 0.07% for women) is far below the expected 2.58% epidemiological prevalence. This gap — roughly 10x underdiagnosis — is the context that makes DHT-based diagnosis tools worth pursuing at all.

**Open Questions:**
- The review finds inconsistent clinical efficacy results across DHIs — is this primarily a study design problem (no standardized outcomes), a heterogeneity problem (ADHD is too heterogeneous for one-size interventions), or a genuine efficacy problem?
- How do the "Drive Clinical Management" DHTs (ML on CPT/neuroimaging/VR data) fit into a clinical pathway where diagnosis relies on subjective clinical interview? Would a clinician actually act on ML output?
- The review ends at December 2025 — how has the rapid scaling of LLM-based tools since then changed the landscape? Are there DHTs using LLMs for ADHD support that postdate this review?
