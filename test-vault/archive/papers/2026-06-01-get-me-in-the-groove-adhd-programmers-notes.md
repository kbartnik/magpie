## Deep Read

**Key Insight:** ADHD programmers are **1.8–4.4× more likely** to struggle with all measured challenges — but non-ADHD programmers also find these challenges difficult (just less frequently). This means organizational accommodations have *generalizable* benefits: you don't have to frame them as disability accommodations to justify them to management.

**What Surprised Me:** The replication package (GitHub, public) and the r/ADHD_Programmers analysis (61,000 members, first academic study of this forum). The anonymity of the subreddit allows disclosure that workplace surveys can't capture — only 1 of 9 neurodivergent programmers who wanted accommodations actually requested them, because the risks are too high.

**Key Findings:**
- 10.6% of programmers have ADHD (Stack Overflow Developer Survey 2022 — N=~70,000)
- Hardest challenges by SE task: time management and *design* (not just implementation)
- Creative strengths validated objectively: ADHD programmers scored higher on divergent thinking tests with medium-to-large effect size
- Accommodation request rate: extremely low due to disclosure risk and bureaucratic friction

**Open Questions:**
- Time management and design are identified as worst, but which specific aspects of design? (Architecture? Estimating scope? Deciding between options?) The breakdown would matter for tool design.
- How do ADHD programmer challenges interact with team composition? (The diverse-teaming productivity literature suggests ADHD diversity increases productivity — how is this mediated?)
- The paper advocates "interventions that support without requiring disclosure" — what does this look like concretely at the organizational level?
