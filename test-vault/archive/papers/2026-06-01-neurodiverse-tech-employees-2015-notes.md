## Deep Read

**Key Insight:** The first systematic study of neurodiverse *software developers specifically* (not just adults with ASD/ADHD generally). The Microsoft Research team found that neurodiverse tech workers face challenges that are distinct from those described in general employment literature — the specificity of software engineering work (code review, debugging, open-plan environments, estimation uncertainty) interacts with neurodivergence in particular ways.

**What Surprised Me:** The study recruited through an internal Microsoft email list for employees with autism *or* family members with autism — meaning the participant pool was self-selected and likely skewed toward higher-functioning, higher-disclosure individuals. The neurodiverse survey respondents (N=59) vs. neurotypical (N=781) comparison likely underestimates the real prevalence and severity, since those who have not disclosed are not represented.

**Open Questions:**
- How do the findings from 2015 hold up in 2025, when remote work has normalized and AI coding tools have changed the SE workflow substantially?
- The paper focuses primarily on ASD; how do ADHD-specific challenges in software engineering differ from ASD-specific challenges, and are they being conflated under "neurodiversity"?
- What organizational changes (not just individual accommodations) would the data suggest — are there patterns in what managers and team leads could do structurally?
