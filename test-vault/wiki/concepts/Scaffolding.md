# Scaffolding

Support calibrated to the learner's Zone of Proximal Development that gradually fades as competence grows. Named for the construction metaphor: scaffolding is temporary; when the building can stand alone, the scaffold comes down.

## Characteristics of Effective Scaffolding

- **Contingent:** Responds to the learner's current performance, not a fixed curriculum
- **Fading:** Reduces support systematically as the learner demonstrates competence
- **Transfers control:** The explicit goal is learner independence; support that persists beyond its utility creates dependence

## Worked Examples

Worked examples are highly effective for novices: showing the complete solution process reduces the cognitive load of problem-solving, leaving working memory available for schema acquisition. When novices attempt problems without examples, cognitive load from managing the problem-solving process crowds out the schema-building they need.

## The Expertise Reversal Effect

As competence grows, worked examples become **less effective** than independent problem-solving. The support that helps novices burdens experts — they now have the schema to solve problems independently, and the worked example imposes redundant processing.

Good instructional design fades scaffolding as expertise grows. A single scaffold can simultaneously help novices and burden advanced learners in the same class.

## Cognitive Apprenticeship

Collins, Brown, and Newman's model applies scaffolding to complex cognitive skills using craft apprenticeship structure: modeling, coaching, scaffolding, articulation, reflection, exploration.

The key move: make expert thinking visible. Experts use tacit knowledge accumulated through experience. Apprenticeship makes this explicit — the expert thinks aloud, the learner observes the *process*, not just the product.

## Connections

- [[Zone of Proximal Development]] — scaffolding is the mechanism that makes within-ZPD tasks accessible; it provides the "more capable other" support Vygotsky described
- [[Cognitive Load Theory]] — worked examples reduce cognitive load for novices (removing problem-solving load); the expertise reversal effect is CLT's prediction about when worked examples stop helping
- [[Deliberate Practice]] — scaffolding and deliberate practice both require calibrated challenge; both fade as competence grows
- [[Intrinsic Motivation]] — effective scaffolding supports competence (achievable challenge) and autonomy (fades toward independence); SDT explains why fading scaffolds work
- [[Executive Function]] — scaffolding substitutes for executive function the learner hasn't developed; external scaffolding and external EF support are structurally similar
