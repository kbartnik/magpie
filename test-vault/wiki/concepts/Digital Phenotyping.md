# Digital Phenotyping

The inference of health status and behavioral characteristics from data generated by digital devices — smartphones, wearables, keyboards, apps — through passive (non-intrusive) observation of everyday behavior. The term was coined to distinguish this approach from active clinical assessments.

> "Almost any personal data is potentially health data." — Perez-Pozuelo et al. 2021

## What It Is

Digital devices now generate continuous streams of behavioral data as a side effect of use:
- **Smartphone:** GPS location, app usage patterns, call/text metadata, accelerometer, microphone (acoustic features, not transcription), screen time
- **Wearables:** heart rate, HRV, skin conductance, skin temperature, accelerometer, sleep stages
- **Computer:** keystroke dynamics, mouse movement, active window, idle periods, scroll behavior
- **Environment:** ambient light sensors, Bluetooth proximity, ambient audio features

These streams can be processed individually or fused to infer health states — mood, stress, cognitive load, sleep quality, attention level — with accuracy comparable to clinical assessments in some domains.

## The Sensing Modality Landscape (Nepal et al. 2025)

A comprehensive survey of passive sensing for workplace wellbeing identified the following modality-outcome relationships:

| Outcome | Key sensing modalities |
|---|---|
| Stress / anxiety | HR, HRV, electrodermal activity, keyboard/mouse activity, behavioral logs |
| Sleep | HR, sleep duration/efficiency, phone usage, movement |
| Mood / affect | Phone usage, speech acoustic features (no transcription), activity |
| Social / team dynamics | Bluetooth proximity, communication metadata |
| Cognitive load | Keystroke, mouse, eye-tracking, physiological arousal |
| Physical activity | Accelerometer, step count, HR |

**Surprising result:** Ambient audio features (speech rate, turn-taking, ambient noise level) predict stress and mood at work without capturing identifiable speech content. Privacy-preserving acoustic sensing is viable.

## RADAR-Base: The Infrastructure Platform

RADAR-base (Rashid et al. 2024) is an open-source platform for longitudinal wearable + smartphone data collection for mental health research. Architecture:
- **Data collection layer** — device-specific SDKs (Fitbit, Empatica, Android passive monitoring)
- **Management layer** — data storage, participant management, quality monitoring
- **Analysis layer** — pipelines for feature extraction and model training

The platform makes multi-modal passive sensing research reproducible without each team reinventing infrastructure from scratch. Currently deployed for depression, psychosis, and chronic disease monitoring; adaptable to ADHD.

**Key enabler:** RADAR-base enables ecological validity at scale — monitoring in "free-living" conditions (real world) rather than controlled labs. This addresses the chronic problem of psychiatric research findings that don't transfer outside the clinic.

## Governance: The Core Problem

Digital phenotyping creates a **data type mismatch**: behavioral data is collected under one consent framework (usage analytics, productivity monitoring, health/fitness tracking) but generates inferences that belong to a different, more sensitive category (medical diagnoses, psychiatric conditions).

**The genomic parallel (Perez-Pozuelo et al. 2021):** Genomic sequencing revealed that what looks like background biological data is deeply sensitive health information with implications for family members and insurance. Digital behavioral data has the same property: it looks like usage data but encodes health status. The governance model needed is similar in kind.

**Proposed principles (Perez-Pozuelo et al.):**
1. Any data that can generate health inferences should be treated as health data under the appropriate regulatory framework
2. Consent should be specific to the inference use case, not just the data collection
3. Individuals should have the right to know what inferences have been drawn from their behavioral data
4. Access controls and data minimization principles from medical data governance should apply

**The ADHD-specific tension:** Employers increasingly deploy productivity monitoring tools that collect keystroke, mouse, and activity data. These systems could, in principle, generate ADHD biomarker signals without explicit consent or even awareness. The employee has not disclosed; the employer is not trying to diagnose — but the inference is structurally possible.

## Relationship to Harness Engineering

Digital phenotyping is the **memory and observability** dimensions of harness engineering applied to human behavioral data rather than AI system logs. Just as a harness monitors model behavior to detect drift, a digital phenotyping system monitors human behavior to detect health state.

The harness engineering principles — instrumentation, inference, intervention — map directly:
- Instrumentation: passive sensing infrastructure (RADAR-base, wearables, keyboard tracking)
- Inference: model layer (digital biomarkers, feature extraction)
- Intervention: feedback/adaptation loop (digital therapeutics, support tools like Tether)
