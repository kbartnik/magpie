# Metacognition

Cognition about cognition — the ability to monitor and regulate one's own thinking. The executive control layer of learning.

## Two Components

**Metacognitive knowledge:** What you know about how cognition works.
- *Person knowledge:* your own cognitive strengths and weaknesses
- *Task knowledge:* what a given task demands
- *Strategy knowledge:* which strategies work for which tasks

**Metacognitive regulation:** Using metacognitive knowledge to control learning.
- *Planning:* selecting strategies, allocating time and attention before beginning
- *Monitoring:* tracking comprehension and performance during a task
- *Evaluation:* assessing outcomes and adjusting strategy after a task

## The Calibration Problem

Calibration is the correspondence between a learner's confidence and their actual performance. Well-calibrated learners know what they know and don't know. Poorly-calibrated learners overestimate their knowledge — a failure of monitoring.

**The Dunning-Kruger effect** is a calibration failure: people with low competence are systematically overconfident; people with high competence are systematically underconfident. The mechanism: developing competence in a domain requires building the same cognitive machinery needed to *evaluate* performance in that domain. Beginners lack the schema to recognize good performance.

**Practical consequence:** Self-assessment is a poor substitute for external assessment in early skill acquisition. Tests, code review, teacher feedback — external signals — are necessary to bootstrap accurate metacognitive knowledge.

## Metacognition and Retrieval Practice

Passive re-reading produces **fluency** — the feeling of knowing — without accurate calibration. The reader recognizes the material, which feels like knowledge. Retrieval practice breaks this illusion: failing to retrieve material you thought you knew is accurate metacognitive feedback that re-reading cannot provide.

## Development and ADHD

Metacognitive ability develops through adolescence into early adulthood, tracking executive function and prefrontal cortex maturation. ADHD disrupts metacognitive regulation specifically: impaired monitoring means less accurate self-assessment; impaired executive function means the regulation cycle doesn't complete.
