# Intrinsic Motivation

Engaging in an activity for its own inherent satisfaction — curiosity, challenge, aesthetic pleasure. Self-Determination Theory (Deci & Ryan) frames intrinsic motivation as produced by three basic psychological needs: **competence**, **autonomy**, and **relatedness**.

## The Three Basic Needs

**Competence:** Feeling effective — mastering challenging tasks, producing desired outcomes. Undermined by tasks too easy (no growth signal) or too hard (repeated failure). Aligns with the Zone of Proximal Development.

**Autonomy:** Feeling self-authored — acting from personal values, not external pressure. Autonomy is not independence; it's the sense that behavior is volitional.

**Relatedness:** Feeling connected — experiencing warm, caring relationships. Less directly tied to learning outcomes but foundational for trust in instructional relationships.

## Extrinsic Motivation and Internalization

Extrinsic motivation exists on a continuum from controlled to self-determined:

| Type | Character | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| External regulation | "For the reward / to avoid punishment" | Grade chasing |
| Introjected | "I'd feel guilty otherwise" | Shame-driven study |
| Identified | "It's important to my goals" | Career relevance |
| Integrated | "It reflects my values" | Functionally intrinsic |

Integrated extrinsic motivation is functionally similar to intrinsic motivation — it's fully internalized. The goal of instruction is to support internalization, not simply to make learning "fun."

## The Over-Justification Effect

When extrinsic rewards are applied to already-interesting activities, intrinsic motivation decreases. Rewards shift the perceived locus of causality from internal to external. **Controlling language** (you *must*, you *should*, you *have to*) has the same effect even without material rewards.

**Informational feedback** ("here's how you can improve") supports competence without undermining autonomy. The same surface praise can support or undermine intrinsic motivation depending on whether it carries a controlling message.

## Connections

- [[Autonomy]] — autonomy need is one of the three basic needs; intrinsic motivation requires autonomy as a supporting condition
- [[Self-Regulated Learning]] — intrinsic motivation sustains the self-regulation cycle; extrinsic motivation can initiate it but rarely maintains effortful monitoring
- [[Deliberate Practice]] — deliberate practice requires sustained intrinsic motivation (or highly internalized extrinsic motivation) to maintain the effort it demands
- [[Scaffolding]] — effective scaffolding supports competence (achievable challenge) and autonomy (fades toward independence); SDT explains why fading scaffolds work
- [[ADHD]] — ADHD's dopamine dysregulation disrupts the reward system that the competence need taps; external structure may be more necessary than SDT's autonomy-support framework assumes
