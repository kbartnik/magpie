# Self-Regulated Learning

Setting learning goals, monitoring progress, adjusting strategies when progress stalls, and evaluating outcomes. The plan-monitor-evaluate-adjust cycle is what distinguishes effective from ineffective learners given identical study time.

## The Cycle

1. **Plan** — set a learning goal; select a strategy; allocate time and attention
2. **Monitor** — track whether the strategy is working; detect comprehension failures
3. **Evaluate** — assess what was learned; compare to the goal
4. **Adjust** — revise strategy for the next attempt based on evaluation

The cycle distinguishes self-regulated learners from time-on-task learners who complete study sessions without meaningful feedback on whether learning occurred.

## It's Trainable

Self-regulation is not a fixed trait. Explicit instruction in metacognitive strategies improves outcomes:
- Prompting self-explanation immediately after learning ("in your own words...")
- Calibration feedback ("you said 80% confident; you were right 60% of the time")
- Worked examples of what good performance looks like

The instruction must be domain-specific. Generic "think about your thinking" advice transfers poorly.

## What Derails the Cycle

- **No monitoring:** studying without checking whether material was understood
- **Inaccurate monitoring:** fluency from re-reading is mistaken for knowledge
- **No evaluation:** session ends without comparing outcomes to goals
- **No adjustment:** same strategy repeated despite poor outcomes

ADHD specifically disrupts the monitoring and evaluation steps through executive function deficits.

## Connections

- [[Metacognition]] — self-regulation is metacognition applied to a goal; metacognitive knowledge enables planning; metacognitive regulation is the cycle itself
- [[Deliberate Practice]] — deliberate practice is a form of self-regulated learning specialized for skill acquisition at the limits of competence
- [[Intrinsic Motivation]] — intrinsic motivation sustains the self-regulation cycle across sessions; extrinsic motivation can initiate it but rarely maintains the effortful monitoring component
- [[Executive Function]] — the self-regulation cycle is executive function applied to learning; impaired EF means the cycle doesn't complete
