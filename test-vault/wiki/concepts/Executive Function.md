# Executive Function

The brain's self-management system — the set of cognitive processes that regulate attention, behavior, and emotion toward goal-directed activity.

## Three Core Components

| Component | Function | Neural Basis |
|---|---|---|
| **Inhibitory control** | Suppress prepotent responses | Right inferior frontal cortex, basal ganglia |
| **Working memory updating** | Hold and manipulate information actively | Dorsolateral PFC, parietal cortex |
| **Cognitive flexibility** | Shift between mental sets and strategies | Anterior cingulate cortex, lateral PFC |

All three depend on the [[Frontostriatal Circuit]] via dopamine-mediated loops.

## Hot vs Cool EF

**Cool EF:** Emotionally neutral tasks (card sorting, verbal fluency). Measured in lab settings.

**Hot EF:** Emotionally charged situations (delay of gratification, impulsive decisions, conflict). ADHD impairs hot EF more than cool EF — explains why ADHD individuals can sometimes perform well in structured lab tasks but fail in real-world emotionally salient situations.

## Development

EF develops from infancy through young adulthood. PFC maturation is not complete until mid-20s. Sensitive to chronic stress (cortisol impairs PFC), play, and bilingualism.

## EF as Predictor

Childhood self-control predicts adult health, wealth, and legal outcomes more strongly than IQ or socioeconomic status (Moffitt et al. 2011, n=1000, birth to age 32).
