# Race Class Narrative (RCN)

A rigorously researched messaging framework developed by Ian Haney López, Heather McGhee, and Anat Shenker-Osorio for building cross-racial coalitions around progressive economic policy. Implemented by We Make the Future Action.

## Core Argument

The wealthy and powerful use **strategic racism** — deliberately stoking racial resentment — to divide working-class people across racial lines and prevent coalitions that would threaten elite economic interests. RCN names this mechanism explicitly and uses it to build solidarity.

The counter-intuitive finding: **color-blind messaging fails**. Avoiding race in progressive messaging leaves the right's racial scapegoating narratives unopposed. Naming race explicitly ("white, Black, or brown," not "everyone") is *more* persuasive across demographics, not less.

## The Template Message

> America's strength comes from our ability to work together — bringing together people from different places and of different races into a whole. For this to be a place where everyone can thrive, we cannot let the 1% and the politicians they pay for divide us against each other based on what someone looks like, where they come from, or how much money they have.

## Evidence of Effectiveness

| Campaign | Context | Result |
|----------|---------|--------|
| Greater Than Fear (MN 2018) | Anti-immigrant/Islamophobic rhetoric | MN House flipped blue, 4 statewide offices flipped |
| Deep canvassing (PA/MI/NC 2019) | Immigrant healthcare coverage | -3% Trump support, +20% support for immigrant healthcare |
| Missouri Medicaid 2020 | Expand coverage to 230,000 | Ballot measure passed |
| Wisconsin GOTV 2020 | Returned ballots and early voting | Positive impact on participation |

## Deep Canvassing

The RCN is most effective when deployed through **deep canvassing** — longer, emotionally candid conversations where canvassers ask voters to share personally significant experiences, rather than standard scripted door-knocking. The PA/MI/NC results produced a larger shift than the total 2012→2016 partisan vote-share change.

## Relationship to Movement Building

RCN is a messaging tool within the broader project of building multiracial coalitions for structural change. It works alongside [[Community Self-Defense]] and organizational capacity — naming the mechanism (elite strategic racism) that the movement is fighting.
