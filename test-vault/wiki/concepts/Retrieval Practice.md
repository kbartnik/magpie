# Retrieval Practice

Testing yourself on material produces more durable retention than re-reading the same material. The act of *retrieving* a memory strengthens it in ways that re-exposure does not.

## Why It Works

Memory is reconstructive, not reproductive. Each retrieval re-encodes the memory, integrating it with current knowledge and strengthening the retrieval pathway. The effort of retrieval is the mechanism of learning — not the recognition of familiarity.

Passive re-reading produces **fluency** — the feeling of knowing. Fluency is unreliable: it's recognition, not recall. The two are different memory systems.

## Forms of Retrieval Practice

- Flashcards (Anki, physical cards)
- Practice problems attempted before checking answers
- The Feynman technique: explain without notes
- Self-quizzing after reading
- Teaching the material to someone else

The form matters less than whether retrieval actually occurs.

## Retrieval Can Strengthen Incorrect Memories

A risk: if you practice retrieving incorrect information, you strengthen the incorrect memory. External feedback (correct answers) after each retrieval attempt is important.
