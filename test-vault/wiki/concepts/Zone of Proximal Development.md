# Zone of Proximal Development

Lev Vygotsky's model of the gap between what a learner can accomplish independently and what they can accomplish with guidance from a more capable other. Learning happens in the zone just above independent competence — not below it, not far above it.

## Three Zones

| Zone | Description | Result |
|------|-------------|--------|
| **Below ZPD** | Tasks the learner can complete independently | Fluency, not learning |
| **Within ZPD** | Achievable with guidance | Productive learning |
| **Above ZPD** | Beyond current capability even with help | Frustration without learning |

A task that a learner can complete independently is not a **learning** task — it's a **performance** task. Learning requires operating within the ZPD.

## Why It Matters for Instruction

Effective instruction keeps learners operating within the ZPD. This requires knowing where the learner currently is — which requires ongoing assessment, not a fixed curriculum. One-size-fits-all instruction will place different learners in different zones simultaneously.

## ZPD and Deliberate Practice

Ericsson's deliberate practice framework converges on the same principle from a different direction: practice must be at the limit of competence, with support and feedback. The ZPD names the zone; deliberate practice names the structure of practice within it.

## Productive Failure

Kapur's finding: learners who attempt problems before instruction (and fail) learn more from subsequent instruction than learners who receive instruction first. The failed attempt generates need-for-knowledge that makes subsequent instruction more effective.

This seems to contradict the ZPD (operating above the ZPD causes frustration), but the resolution is timing: a bounded failure attempt activates prior knowledge and creates cognitive contrast that makes subsequent instruction more salient.

## Connections

- [[Scaffolding]] — scaffolding is the mechanism for operating within the ZPD; it provides the "more capable other" support that makes within-ZPD tasks achievable
- [[Deliberate Practice]] — ZPD and deliberate practice converge: both require operating at the edge of competence with support and feedback
- [[Cognitive Load Theory]] — within-ZPD tasks calibrate cognitive load to the learner; above-ZPD tasks produce extraneous load without germane load benefit
- [[Intrinsic Motivation]] — SDT's competence need aligns with ZPD: optimal challenges (within ZPD) support intrinsic motivation; too-easy or too-hard challenges undermine it
