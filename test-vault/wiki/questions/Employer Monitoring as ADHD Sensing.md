# Employer Monitoring as ADHD Sensing

*When employer productivity monitoring infrastructure can passively infer ADHD status, what governance frameworks apply — and does collected data become protected health information?*

Nepal et al. (2025) demonstrate that passive workplace sensing (keystroke dynamics, mouse movement, Bluetooth proximity, app usage) can predict cognitive states associated with ADHD. Employer productivity monitoring systems that deploy this infrastructure may — unintentionally or intentionally — function as passive ADHD diagnostic sensing.

The governance questions are unresolved: Does employer-collected productivity data become health data if it is used (or could be used) for disability inference? Do workers have meaningful consent over sensing systems whose ADHD-diagnostic potential wasn't disclosed at deployment? Does ADA/employment law protect against discriminatory use of inferred ADHD status? What triggers the duty to provide accommodation — formal diagnosis, informal inference, or employee self-disclosure?

Perez-Pozuelo 2021 establishes the principle that "any data is health data" in digital phenotyping, but employer-specific legal implications remain untested in court.
