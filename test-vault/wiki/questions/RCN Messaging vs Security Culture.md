# RCN Messaging vs Security Culture

*How should organizers balance the RCN requirement for explicit public messaging about race against security culture's need to minimize strategic transparency?*

RCN works through explicit public messaging: the explicit naming of race is the mechanism that makes it persuasive (Mendelberg's research). An organizer deploying RCN messaging publicly is identifiable as someone trained in RCN strategy, which reveals organizational affiliations and strategic orientation to adversaries.

Security culture recommends minimizing public attribution of strategy. An adversary who knows you're using RCN can prepare counter-messaging, identify your strategic advisors, or use your public framing to misrepresent your goals. The tension: strategic effectiveness (explicit public messaging) versus operational security (not advertising your theory of change).

A partial resolution: RCN training and strategy development happens in secure settings; RCN messaging is deployed publicly without attributing it to RCN. But this requires discipline and means organizers can't publicly credit the framework that's producing results, reducing its spread.
