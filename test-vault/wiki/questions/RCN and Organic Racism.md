# RCN and Organic Racism

*Is the Race Class Narrative framework effective against organic (non-elite-manufactured) racism, or does its mechanism only work against strategically deployed racism?*

The RCN framework is designed to counter elite-sponsored racism — the strategic use of racial division by wealthy actors to prevent cross-racial working-class coalitions. The theoretical mechanism: explicitly naming race, exposing the elite manipulation, and redirecting toward shared economic interests. The deep canvassing evidence supports this.

The harder question: what about racism that emerges from community-level competition for scarce resources, historical resentment, or cultural anxiety that predates elite manipulation? The RCN two-step origin story (elite → community) doesn't fit organic racism. Explicitly naming race in an RCN conversation may not help — and might reinforce — racial division that arose from lived community experience rather than strategic manipulation.

Whether RCN messaging is effective, ineffective, or counterproductive against organic racism is not addressed in Fassia & Simms. The framework's evidentiary base is specifically about countering elite-deployed messaging, which may not generalize.
