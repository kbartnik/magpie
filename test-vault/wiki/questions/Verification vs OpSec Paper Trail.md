# Verification vs OpSec Paper Trail

*How should activists conducting exposure research balance evidentiary verification standards against the OpSec liability of maintaining a verification paper trail?*

The "Exposing Fascists: Best Practices" guide emphasizes cross-referencing and confirmation before publication — evidentiary standards that reduce the risk of misidentification and legal liability. But the verification process creates a paper trail: saved screenshots, notes, communications with sources, search histories, and intermediate working documents. If this documentation is discovered — through a device seizure, an account compromise, or a civil subpoena — it reveals research methods, sources, and potentially the identities of researchers.

The tension is structural: verification requires accumulating evidence; evidence is an OpSec liability. Partial mitigations: ephemeral working environments (Tails OS, fresh VMs), encrypted note storage with separate keys from communication keys, source compartmentalization so no single document links multiple research threads. But each mitigation has friction costs and failure modes.

No clean resolution exists. The correct balance depends on threat model: a researcher facing potential doxxing by organized adversaries needs heavier OpSec than one documenting local low-sophistication actors.
