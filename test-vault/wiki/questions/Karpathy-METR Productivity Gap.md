# Karpathy-METR Productivity Gap

*What explains the gap between Karpathy's elite-practitioner productivity claims and the METR RCT's finding that experienced developers are 19% slower with AI?*

Karpathy (AI Ascent 2026) demonstrates building full apps in hours and argues agentic engineering enables dramatically faster, higher-quality work. The METR RCT (Becker et al. 2025) measured experienced open-source developers using Cursor Pro + Claude 3.5/3.7 on real tasks: they were **19% slower** with AI than without, while predicting they'd been 20% faster — a 39-point misperception.

These aren't directly comparable, but the gap is large enough to demand explanation. Candidate hypotheses: selection bias in Karpathy's demos (only showing wins); task type matters (greenfield vs. incremental open-source maintenance); specific AI tooling experience (fluency with agentic workflows, not just programming experience); or the RCT's task selection was unrepresentative of tasks where AI helps most.

None of these hypotheses has been tested directly. If gains are concentrated at a small elite, the economic and social implications differ significantly from broad-based gains.
