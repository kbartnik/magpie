# Ghosts Framing Engineering Prescriptions

*What concrete engineering practices does Karpathy's 'ghosts not animals' framing actually change, beyond what 'probabilistic pattern machine' already implies?*

Karpathy's "ghosts not animals" framing — LLMs as "jagged, statistical, summoned entities" that aren't intelligences of any familiar type — is intended to shift how engineers work with them. But its specific engineering prescriptions remain underdeveloped. "Treat it like a ghost" might mean: don't expect consistent behavior across sessions; don't anthropomorphize failure modes; don't rely on it for verification. These prescriptions also follow from the simpler "probabilistic pattern machine" framing in [[LLM Mental Model]].

The question is what "ghost" adds. A ghost analogy might specifically warn against the anthropomorphic trap that the pattern-machine framing doesn't prevent — engineers who understand the statistical mechanics still collaborate with the model as if it had intent, goals, and memory. The ghost framing may be targeting a psychological habit rather than a technical misconception. But if so, the prescription is still unspecified.
