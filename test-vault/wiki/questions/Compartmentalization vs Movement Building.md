# Compartmentalization vs Movement Building

*Is the tension between strict security culture compartmentalization and the openness required for broad movement-building resolvable, or does it require accepting one as a permanent constraint on the other?*

Strict compartmentalization — need-to-know information, cell structures, limited cross-organizational knowledge — conflicts structurally with movement building, which requires public visibility, accessible entry points, and shared public identity. You cannot recruit widely into a structure you cannot describe.

The implicit two-tier resolution in the literature: a public-facing layer (explicitly non-secure) and a secure operational layer. But the boundary between them is not defined in the primary sources, and the public layer is not acknowledged as a surveillance surface that feeds the operational layer's threat model. Smartphone-era surveillance makes the public layer more porous than it appeared in 2004: attending a public event, being photographed at a march, or liking a post creates metadata that can connect the public and operational layers.

The honest position may be that this tension cannot be fully resolved — every movement accepts some risk in exchange for scale. The question is which risks are acceptable and how they're managed, not whether they can be eliminated.
