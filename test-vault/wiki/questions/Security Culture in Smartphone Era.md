# Security Culture in Smartphone Era

*Does the 2004 CrimethInc. security culture framework adequately address smartphone-era surveillance, or does ubiquitous location and metadata tracking require a fundamentally updated threat model?*

The 2004 framework was designed against infiltrator threats: compartmentalization makes human informants irrelevant because they can only report on what they know, and need-to-know structures limit that. The threat model has changed. Location data, communication metadata, financial transactions, and social graph analysis allow surveillance systems to reconstruct organizational structures without any human informant. Smartphones are passive surveillance devices accompanying activists everywhere.

The framework may still be foundational without being sufficient: if compartmentalization is practiced, digital tracking reveals *who you meet with* but not *what was said*. The WIRED guide's practical tooling (Signal, Faraday bags, location minimization) is the 2024 update to the 2004 framework's principles. The question is whether these together are sufficient against a state-level adversary with subpoena power over telcos and platform companies.
